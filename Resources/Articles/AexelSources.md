# Aexel Sources

The Darkness article has long described the qualitative picture: antimatter creates aexels, this creation causes the apparent expansion of the universe and the same antimatter dust pushing on galaxies from the outside accounts for the phenomena attributed to dark matter.

But the question remained: does this mechanism produce the right quantitative behavior?  Specifically, can it reproduce the observed expansion rate?

The Floating Leaf article showed how to calculate the inward flow of aexels toward a concentration of matter.  The escape velocity at radius r gives the flow speed and plugging that into the Lorentz factor recovers the Schwarzschild gravitational dilation.  The calculation for antimatter is the mirror image.  Aexels flow outward from a concentration of antimatter at a velocity that also follows from the escape velocity formula: $v = \sqrt{2GM/r}$.

The key question is: what is the rate of expansion at a distance r from an antimatter source?  The outward velocity is $\sqrt{2GM/r}$.  But we are interested in the expansion rate per unit distance, which is this velocity divided by r: $\sqrt{2GM} / r^{3/2}$.

This falls off with distance from a point source, which does not look like the uniform expansion we observe.  But the antimatter of Universe X is not a point source.  It is a diffuse dust spread uniformly between the galaxies.  For a uniform density ρ, the mass within a sphere of radius r is $M = \rho \frac{4}{3}\pi r^3$.  Substituting this into the expression:

$$
v/r = \sqrt{2 G \rho \tfrac{4}{3} \pi r^2} \,/\, r = \sqrt{\tfrac{8 G \rho \pi}{3}}
$$

The r cancels.  The expansion rate per unit distance is a constant, independent of r.  If the antimatter dust has uniform density, the expansion rate is the same everywhere.

This is the Friedmann equation for a flat universe: $H^2 = 8\pi G\rho/3$.  It falls straight out of the Universe X machinery with nothing more than undergrad algebra.

One can go further.  In Universe X there is no real expansion; the universe is steady state and the redshift is caused by photons passing through the antimatter dust.  The actual radius of the universe is therefore closer to 13.8 billion light-years (age times c) rather than the 46.5 billion light-year comoving radius that accounts for expansion.  Using the ordinary matter mass of approximately 1.5 × 10⁵³ kg (which equals the antimatter mass in Universe X) spread over this smaller volume, one can calculate the density and plug it into the Friedmann equation.

The result is H₀ ≈ 92 km/s/Mpc.  The observed value is between 67 and 73 km/s/Mpc.  Given the rough estimates involved, arriving within 30% using nothing but the basic mechanisms of Universe X is a striking result.

No dark matter.  No dark energy.  No inflation.  No mysterious initial conditions.  And perhaps speculatively, the Cosmic Microwave Background may not be an echo of a Big Bang at all, but rather light reflecting off the boundary of the universe.
