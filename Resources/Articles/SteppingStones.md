# Stepping Stones

Throughout this exploration there has been a phenomenon that has occurred numerous times and I suspect will continue to occur.  A conceptual breakthrough happens that is approximately correct and extremely helpful in moving the concepts forward, but that ultimately proves to be precisely incorrect.

These are not open questions that were eventually answered or fleeting ideas that were quickly discarded.  These are concepts that clicked, that felt like answers, that were used productively for extended periods of time and that were ultimately refined by something deeper.

It is important to be mindful that such refinement is possible and perhaps likely with each of the concepts arrived at here.  I would like to make this a living document that catalogs this phenomenon as it occurs.

## One Aexel Per Tic is the Speed of Light

From the very beginning, the speed of light was described as one aexel per tic; a signal jumping from one aexel to its neighbor in one unit of time.  This was clean, intuitive and immediately explained why nothing could go faster than light.  But as discussed in the Speed of Light article, the actual speed of light in Universe X depends on the geometry of the lattice, the angle of traversal and the internal mechanics of the propagating entity itself.  One aexel per tic is the lattice speed, not necessarily the speed of light.  This concept is further explored in the Speed of Light article.

## Mass Destroys Aexels

The progression here has been substantial.  Initially, it was mass that destroyed aexels; this was the mechanism underpinning gravity.  Then it was realized that it was not mass specifically but rather translation at the speed of light that pulled on aexels.  Then with the concept of hyle, it became clear that hyle was the entity responsible.  And finally, with the accelerant concept, it was realized that hyle does not destroy aexels directly at all; hyle destroys accelerant.  The velocity of the accelerant then induces an acceleration on the aexels which can ultimately lead to their squishing under pressure.  Each step was productive and approximately correct, but each was ultimately refined.

## Squishing is the Warping of Space

Early on, the compression of aexels near massive objects was identified as the mechanism behind the 'warping of space' described by General Relativity.  The aexels were getting smooshed and this smooshing was the physical reality behind curved spacetime.  But as the understanding deepened it became clear that the aether itself does not warp at all.  The apparent warping is entirely due to length contraction caused by the flow of aexels at each point.  Meter sticks at those points are contracted, making distances appear different than they are, but space and even the aether itself remain flat.
