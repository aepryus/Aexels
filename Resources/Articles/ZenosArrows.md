# Zeno's Arrows

In approximately the 5th century BC, the philosopher Zeno of Elea posed a deceptively simple paradox.  Consider an arrow in flight.  At any single instant of time, the arrow occupies a space equal to itself.  It is indistinguishable from a stationary arrow occupying that same space.  If at every instant the arrow is indistinguishable from a stationary arrow, then how is motion possible?

For over two thousand years this paradox has resisted a fully satisfying resolution.  But in Universe X, the resolution is immediate and it has profound consequences.

Consider two protons in Universe X.  One is at rest in the aether frame.  The other is translating across the aexels at some velocity.  Are these two protons identical?

They are not.  The moving proton contains more hyle than the stationary one.  It has its intrinsic hyle, the hyle that makes it a proton, but it also has kinetic hyle; additional hyle that is the substance of its motion.  More hyle means a greater pull on the aexels.  More hyle means more gravitation.

And gravitation is not frame dependent.  One cannot simply change one's perspective and make the additional gravitational pull disappear.  The moving proton objectively, physically, measurably gravitates more than the stationary one.

This resolves Zeno's paradox.  The two arrows are not the same.  A frozen snapshot of a moving arrow and a frozen snapshot of a stationary arrow are physically different objects.  The moving arrow contains more hyle.  Zeno, roughly 2,500 years ago, anticipated E=mc².

But this also has a devastating consequence for Special Relativity's principle of reciprocity.  If a moving proton gravitates more than a stationary one and if this gravitation is frame independent, then there is an objective, physical fact about which proton is moving and which is not.  There is a preferred frame.  Two observers cannot both consider themselves to be at rest; the gravitational measurements will tell them who is right.

E=mc² is incompatible with reciprocity.  Once again, from an entirely different direction than the Conspiracy argument, reciprocity is dead.
