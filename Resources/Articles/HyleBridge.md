# Hyle Bridge

Since the development of the E&M simulation in version 4.0, there has been a nagging problem with the hyle exchange mechanism.  The ping / pong handshake works beautifully; the cupola algorithm elegantly solves the problem of blind nodes finding one another and the force direction falls out naturally.  But the actual transfer of hyle between teslons has resisted a clean solution.

The problem is straightforward to state.  When a source teslon receives a pong confirming that a target teslon exists, it releases a photon carrying hyle.  The source recoils.  But the target does not receive that photon and its associated kick until some time later.  The kicks are not synchronized.

For two teslons aligned parallel to the direction of translation, this asymmetry is particularly troubling.  The trailing teslon receives its ping first and reacts first.  The system momentarily violates the balance that should exist between two particles in a shared interaction.

I had long assumed that since the flow of pings and pongs is continuous these out of sync reactions were just edge effects that averaged out.  But as I waded deeper into trying to make the hyle exchange quantitatively work, it became clear that something more fundamental was going on.  The momentum and energy carried by a photon are always equal in natural units.  But the momentum and energy lost by a teslon are never equal.  Local conservation through photon exchange simply was not working out.

The resolution came from an unexpected direction.  What if hyle can move faster than light?  Not through the aether, not as a signal jumping from aexel to aexel, but over the pong bridge itself.

The ping / pong handshake establishes a connection between two teslons.  That bridge, once established, is a direct link.  If hyle can transfer instantaneously over this bridge, then both teslons recoil at the same moment.  The synchronization problem vanishes.

This is a significant departure.  Nothing else in Universe X moves faster than c.  Pings travel at c.  Pongs travel at c.  Photons travel at c.  But hyle, moving over an already established pong bridge, is not a signal propagating through the aether.  It is a transfer along a pre-existing connection.

The implications are substantial.  Entanglement becomes the natural state of any two teslons that have completed a ping / pong handshake.  The apparently spooky correlations observed in quantum experiments are just hyle moving over existing bridges regardless of distance.  Bell's inequality is violated because the pong bridge is a non-local hidden variable and Bell's theorem only rules out *local* hidden variables.

The pong bridge provides a physical, mechanistic basis for the non-local behavior of Quantum Mechanics without abandoning the deterministic, mechanistic aesthetics of Universe X.
